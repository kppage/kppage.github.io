About Me

Hello! My name is Kaitlin Page and I am currently a 4th grade teacher at Willow Springs Elementary School in Wake County. I have been teaching for the last 10 years and during that time I have developed a love of technology integration in the classroom. This passion led me to apply to the Learning Design and Technology program at NCSU in 2019. For the past two years, I have grown my professional knowledge and intend to seek employment as a district technology facilitator upon my graduation in May. My hope is to coach educators in technology integration to increase student achievement and decrease teacher workload.

Favorite Edtech Tools

GSuite

Above all, my favorite edtech tool is GSuite for education. Although there are many tools that are more groundbreaking, I see GSuite as a teacher's "bread and butter". It is easy to learn, use, and implement with students of all ages in the classroom. It not only encourages collaboration between students, but also allows teachers to work smarter, not harder. Some of my favorite activities to implement with GSuite tools are:

  • Interactive learning with Jamboard (especially during breakout rooms during remote learning)
  • Collaborative writing revision with Google Docs
  • Instant feedback on assignments and homework through Google Forms
  • Choose-your-own-adventure math challenges through Google Slides or Google Forms

Nearpod

Nearpod has been a life-saver during COVID-19 remote learning. It allows teachers to create and deliver engaging, interactive lessons for students while also monitoring student engagement and understanding throughout each lesson. A new feature also allows teachers to embed questions within video to ensure students interact with and understanding video content when learning independently. My favoritie features of Nearpod are:

  • Student data pops up in real-time showing the teacher how each student answered questions, allowing for quick intervention as necessary
  • Teachers can control the pace of lessons so students are unable to move ahead until ready
  • There is a vast library of pre-created content allowing teachers to save time

Flipgrid

Flipgrid has been another pivotal tool during remote learning, especially in the elementary classroom. It is a discussion-based platform that allows students and teachers to create and respond to video content. The intuative design and built-in backgrounds/decorations make it easy and fun for students of all ages to engage. The possibilites are endless, but some of my favorite activities are:

  • Book reviews and novel discussions
  • Oral reading fluency recordings and feedback
  • Newscastings integrating science standards
  • Explain-your-thinking math debates
